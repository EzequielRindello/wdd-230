    agrelo, lujan de cuyo

    Though our winery is in a small winemaking town, the Province of Mendoza is the largest winemaking region in South America. Lujan de Cuyo received Argentina's first Appelation d'Origine Controlee for their winemaking prowess of the Malbec grape. Piattelli's Grand Reserve, Premium Reserve Malbec and Rose of Malbec grapes are grown here, and yield some of the best Malbec wines in the market.

    tupungato, west valley

    Travel another 20 miles beyond the Piattelli winery in Agrelo, you will enter Tupungato; at nearly 4,000ft above sea-level, it has a higher elevation than both Agrelo and Mendoza City. Our vineyards in Tupungato grow Piattelli's Premium and Grand Reserve Cabernet Sauvignon grapes as well as the Premium Chardonnay grapes.

    elevation, cafayate

    ELEVATION Cafayate sits at 5600ft (1700m) above sea level, and is arguably the best-known wine region other than Mendoza in Argentina. Cafayate is one of the highest regions in the world that is suitable for viticulture. This high altitude is what defines the terrior of the region and makes it suitable for growing grapes despite its close proximity to the equator.




    Learn more Instagram Facebook

    terroir, cafayate

    The soils in Cafayate consist mostly of free-draining chalky loam and in some areas can be quite rocky. The dry soils cause stress in the vines which causes them to produce less vegetation and not as many grapes. One would think this is a bad thing, but in fact, it proves to be very good and that less, truly is more. As there are fewer grapes, all the work the vines do to get these few grapes the nutrients means the concentration of flavors within the grapes rises.

    ustapallata, mendoza

    Uspallata is an Andean city in Argentina, near the border with Chile. It is known for its nearby desert mountains, such as the Cerro de los Siete Colores and Cerro Tunduqueral, with its Inca petroglyphs. The Bóvedas, egg-shaped clay ovens, were built in the 17th century to process gold and silver from the Paramillos mines. Nearby is the petrified forest of Darwin's Araucarias Paleontological Park.
